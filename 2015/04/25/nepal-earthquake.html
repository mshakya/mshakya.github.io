Help is on the way (How and from Where?)

Apr 25, 2015

I have put together a list of different types of help that are either already in Nepal or on the way. Please help me grow this list by sharing thoughts and links on comment section below.

Although, we cant be there to help, I hope together with our effort we can assure our families, that HELP is ON THE WAY.

I have categorized help (whether its monetary or not) based on their source (country, agencies).

Click here for list of agencies.

List of Countries that have stepped up to help.

Australia

  • Australia is sending in a Crisis Response Team and two humanitarian expert.
  • Australia has pledged $5 million distributed as follows:
    • $2.5 million to assist Australian non-government organisations;
    • $2 million to support United Nations partners; and
    • $0.5 million to support the Australian Red Cross.

My update on the situation in Nepal: http://t.co/5t7shKO6up

— Julie Bishop (@JulieBishopMP) April 26, 2015

Canada

  • Canada is contributing 5,000,000$ and also sending personnel help. link

We are deploying a Canadian Armed Forces Disaster Assistance Response (DART) assessment team to Nepal immediately, in response to the earthquake.

Posted by Jason Kenney on Saturday, April 25, 2015

China

  • China has sent 68-strong China International Search and Rescue Team (CISAR). The team will arrive in Kathmandu midday Sunday (April 26, 2015). See the press release here.

France

  • France is sending 11 rescuers and emergency equipment to Kathmandu on Sunday (April 26, 2015). link

Germany

  • Germany has sent a team of 52 relief workers that include doctors, search experts, rescue dogs. link

India

  • A tweet from Narendra Modi about donation of Rs. 51 lakh by Pujya Morari Bapu. Many thanks to him. As of yet I have not found details on how this money will be used in Nepal.

Received a call from Pujya Morari Bapu. He has donated Rs. 51 lakh for relief work in Nepal. My deepest gratitude to him.

— Narendra Modi (@narendramodi) April 25, 2015
  • Series of tweets from Vikas Swarup of Ministry of External Affairs, India regarding help being deployed to Nepal. A total of 285 NDRF team members have been sent to help with rescue along with 43 tons of relief material, including tents and food link.

285 NDRF team members have been flown in from India in 3 IAF aircrafrts to assist Nepal Army in rescue operations @MEAIndia #NepalEarthquake

Israel

  • Israel also plans to dispatch search and rescue teams and humanitarian aid to Nepal as per Times of Israel.

  • IsraAID also has setup a fund raising page to collect funds. Here is the link.

Italy

  • Italy has made $316,000 available through International Red Cross. See press release here.

Japan

  • Japan is sending around 70 members (on Sunday April 26, 2015) from the National Police Agency, the Fire and Disaster Management Agency and the Japan Coast Guard who will coordinate relief efforts with the Nepalese government.link

Malaysia

  • Malaysia will send 20 medical doctors along with a 30-man rescue team and medicines and medical supplies.link

New Zealand

  • 45 specialised urban search and rescue (USAR) personnel from New Zealand have been sent to Nepal. The crew also includes four dog handlers and are trained in using search and rescue cameras, listening devices and line rescues. link

Pakistan

  • Pakistan is sending in rescue teams, more equipments to help rescue teams, hospital beds, Doctors, and food items.

Poland

  • Poland has sent 81 firefighters of the State Fire Service, 12 search and rescue dogs and 6 doctors of Polish Center for International Aid. link

Russia

  • Russia is getting ready to send more than 50 rescue workers with extensive experience in rescue missions. link

Singapore

  • Sigapore is preparing to send a 55 man search and rescue team in addition to $100,000 contribution towards Singapore Red Cross. See press release here.

South Korea

  • South Korea has pledged 1M$. link.

Sri Lanka

  • An airforce plane carrying four doctors will leave for Nepal on April 26th. Srilankan government will also send dry ration and other essential items by tomorrow (April 27th). link

Scandinavian countries (Norway, Sweden, and Denmark)

  • Scandinavian country will allocate $3.8 million for aid which will be distributed through United Nations and Charitable organizations. See press release here.

United Kingdom

  • UK has sent a team of “eight-strong” team that consist of experts in search and rescue. See press release here.

United States

  • A press release from USAID promising “an initial $1 million in emergency assistance” through Government of Nepal along with disaster response team.link

The U.S. Government has been providing ongoing disaster preparedness support – and #WeStandWithNepal ready to help....
